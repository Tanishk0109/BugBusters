Module 2: Practical Interaction Conversation

Signs for Essential Words

Learn signs for food, colors, and essential items

Food Signs

Apple

Make a fist with your index knuckle extended, then twist it against your cheek.

Bread

Using both hands, make cutting motions as if slicing a loaf of bread.

Water

Form a 'W' with your dominant hand and tap it on your chin twice.

Color Signs

Red

Touch your lips with the index finger of your dominant hand.

Blue

Make a 'B' hand shape and wave it side to side in front of you.

Green

Make a 'G' hand shape and shake it side to side.

Essential Items

Phone

Make a 'Y' hand shape and put it to your ear.

Keys

Mimic turning a key in a lock with your index finger and thumb.

Wallet

Pat your back pocket or hip area where you'd keep a wallet.

Basic Conversation Phrases

Learn essential phrases for everyday communication

Hello

Wave your hand, or sign by touching your forehead with your dominant hand and moving it away.

How are you?

Point to the other person, then make two fists and rotate them in front of your chest.

Thank you

Touch your chin or lips with your fingertips, then move your hand forward and down.

Please

Rub your chest in a circular motion with an open hand.

Sorry

Make a fist and rub it in a circular motion over your chest.

Practice Exercise

Try combining these phrases into a short conversation:

  1. Greet someone (Hello)
  2. Ask how they are (How are you?)
  3. Respond with "Thank you" after they answer
  4. Ask for something using "Please"
  5. If you make a mistake, use "Sorry"

Practice this sequence in front of a mirror, focusing on smooth transitions between signs.

Situational Vocabulary

Learn signs for shopping and transportation scenarios

Shopping Vocabulary

Store

Make an 'S' hand shape and move it in a square shape in front of you.

Money

Rub your thumb against your fingertips, like feeling paper money.

Expensive

Pull your hand away from your chin, closing your fingers as you do.

Cheap

Make a 'Y' hand shape and move it downwards quickly.

Buy

Move a flat hand from your chest outward, as if handing over money.

Sale

Make 'S' hand shapes with both hands and move them down, like price tags dropping.

Transportation Vocabulary

Car

Make 'C' hand shapes with both hands and move them as if steering a car.

Bus

Make 'B' hand shapes with both hands and move them down, like bus doors closing.

Train

Move your index and middle fingers in a line, like train tracks.

Airplane

Make a 'Y' hand shape and move it forward like a plane taking off.

Ticket

Pinch your fingers together and move them across your palm, like tearing a ticket.

Late

Tap your wrist where a watch would be, then point behind you.

Practice Scenario

Imagine you're planning a shopping trip. Practice signing this scenario:

  1. You want to go to the store
  2. You need to take the bus
  3. You hope to find a sale
  4. You have some money to spend
  5. You don't want to be late

Try to use as many of the shopping and transportation signs as you can in this scenario.
